Pollo al Limone

★★★★★

Italian

Prep Time: 15 Cook Time: 20 Difficulty: Medium Servings: 6

Ingredients

2 lbs Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs

1/2 cup All-Purpose Flour (for dredging)

1 tsp Salt

1 tsp Black Pepper

3 tbsp Olive Oil

5 cloves Garlic, minced

2 tbsp Tomato Paste

1/2 cup Chicken Broth (or Dry White Wine)

1 Lemon (Juiced)

1 bag Fresh Spinach (8-10 oz)

1 tbsp Cold Butter (cut into cubes)

Description

A refined Italian scaloppine dish. The secret is dusting the chicken in flour before searing (to create a crust and thicken the sauce) and finishing with cold butter to create a glossy, emulsified lemon-tomato glaze.

Directions

Dredge: Mix flour, salt, and pepper on a plate. Press chicken thighs into the flour to coat, then shake off all excess. (This creates the crust).

Sear: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over Medium-High. Sear chicken for 4 minutes per side until golden. Remove to a plate.

Bloom: Reduce heat to Medium. Add garlic and Tomato Paste to the pan drippings. Fry for 2 minutes until the paste turns dark red (essential for depth).

Glaze: Pour in broth and lemon juice. Scrape up the browned bits. The leftover flour in the pan will instantly thicken this into a sauce.

Simmer: Return chicken to the pan. Simmer for 4–5 minutes until cooked through.

Finish: Turn heat to Low. Stir in the Cold Butter and the fresh spinach. Toss gently until the butter melts (creating a gloss) and spinach is wilted. Serve immediately.
