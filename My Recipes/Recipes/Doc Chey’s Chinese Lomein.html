Doc Chey’s Chinese Lomein

Servings: Per serving: 717 calories Source: ajc.com

Ingredients

1 pound boneless chicken breast

Salt and pepper

1 tablespoon vegetable oil

2 teaspoons minced garlic

1 teaspoon minced ginger

1 cups sliced onions

1 cup sliced carrots

1 cup sliced bok choy

1 cup edamame

4 cups cooked egg noodles

Cantonese Sauce (see recipe)

2 tablespoons sliced scallions

Cantonese Sauce

1/4 cup soy sauce

1/4 cup Shaoxing cooking wine

1/4 cup oyster sauce

2 tablespoons granulated sugar

Directions

Season chicken with salt and pepper. Can be done ahead of time. If made ahead, cover and refrigerate until ready to cook.

Heat a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. When hot, add oil then quickly add chicken and sear until chicken is cooked through, about 3 minutes. Remove chicken and set aside. Add garlic and ginger and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add onions, carrots and cabbage and continue sautéing until vegetables begin to caramelize, about 4 minutes. Add noodles and Cantonese sauce to the vegetable mixture. Add cooked chicken. Continue heating and stirring until everything is even coated and sauce has reduced by half, about 3 minutes. Divide lo mein between serving bowls and garnish with scallions. Serve immediately. Serves 2.

In a medium bowl, whisk together soy sauce, cooking wine, oyster sauce and sugar. Whisk until the sugar dissolves. Set aside. Makes: 3/4 cup

Nutrition

Per serving: 717 calories (percent of calories from fat, 17), 45 grams protein, 104 grams carbohydrates, 10 grams fiber, 13 grams fat (2 grams saturated), 171 milligrams cholesterol, 145 milligrams sodium.
Per serving: (one tablespoon) 15 calories (percent of calories from fat, 1), trace protein, 3 grams carbohydrates, trace fiber, trace fat (no saturated fat), no cholesterol, 383 milligrams sodium.
