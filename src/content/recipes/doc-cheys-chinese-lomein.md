ingredients:
  - 1 pound boneless chicken breast
  - Salt and pepper
  - 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  - 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  - 1 teaspoon minced ginger
  - 1 cups sliced onions
  - 1 cup sliced carrots
  - 1 cup sliced bok choy
  - 1 cup edamame
  - 4 cups cooked egg noodles
  - Cantonese Sauce (see recipe)
  - 2 tablespoons sliced scallions
  - Cantonese Sauce
  - 1/4 cup soy sauce
  - 1/4 cup Shaoxing cooking wine
  - 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  - 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
---

## Directions

1. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Can be done ahead of time. If made ahead, cover and refrigerate until ready to cook.
2. Heat a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. When hot, add oil then quickly add chicken and sear until chicken is cooked through, about 3 minutes. Remove chicken and set aside. Add garlic and ginger and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add onions, carrots and cabbage and continue sautéing until vegetables begin to caramelize, about 4 minutes. Add noodles and Cantonese sauce to the vegetable mixture. Add cooked chicken. Continue heating and stirring until everything is even coated and sauce has reduced by half, about 3 minutes. Divide lo mein between serving bowls and garnish with scallions. Serve immediately. Serves 2.
3. In a medium bowl, whisk together soy sauce, cooking wine, oyster sauce and sugar. Whisk until the sugar dissolves. Set aside. Makes: 3/4 cup
